## ** Overview**

This project explores customer churn behavior in a telecommunications company using structured Excel-based analysis. The goal is to understand why customers leave, which behaviors signal early churn risk, and how service plans and usage patterns influence customer retention. The project walks through Basic, Medium, and Advanced analytical layers, each uncovering deeper insights into customer behaviour and revenue impact.
The analysis is designed to support strategic decision-making by helping the business identify at-risk customers, improve service offerings, and design targeted retention programs.

## ** Key Insights from the Analysis**

* States such as New Jersey, California, Washington, and Maryland display higher churn levels, indicating markets with strong competitive pressure or service dissatisfaction.
* Customers on the International Plan churn at a significantly higher rate, pointing to possible pricing confusion or bill-related dissatisfaction.
* Voice Mail Plan users show much stronger retention, suggesting a higher level of engagement and perceived value.
* Customers making repeated customer service calls show dramatically rising churn rates, highlighting dissatisfaction with issue resolution.
* Day-time usage and charges have the strongest relationship with churn among numeric variables.
* Heavy-usage customers are more prone to churn, likely due to price sensitivity.
* Predictive variables such as international plan status, customer service calls, and day-time usage consistently emerge as top churn drivers.
* Segmentation reveals clear customer groups, allowing targeted intervention strategies.
* High-value customers represent the highest financial impact when they churn, underscoring the need for early retention actions.
